About

Sensory Percussion is creating next-generation electronic drums that captures the true expressive nature of drumming. An overlay on your current acoustic drums, Sensory Percussion lets you map different parts of the drum to any sound desired–from samples and synthesizers to digital audio effects. Sounds follow your playing in real-time–so rather than twiddle knobs and push buttons you can have nuanced control by simply playing the drums.

  More than just triggers

Sensory Percussion understands where and how you are hitting the drum and is sensitive to the most nuanced touches. It follows your playing in a truly natural way. While triggers turn your drum into just a switch for a single sample and are not sensitive enough to capture intricate playing, Sensory Percussion listens to the sound of your drum to capture the essense of your performance beyond just timing and volume.

  Designed for the stage

Sensory Percussion was built for live performance. It has low latency and a user interface designed for the stage. Our proprietary drum sensors capture the sound of your drum while isolating it from ambient noise, making it possible to play in loud environments. Sensory Percussion will allow you to extend your drums with great sounding pre-recorded and mastered drum sounds, but also to experiment with whatever sounds you want, opening the door to a new era of performance and composition for instrumentalists.

  Record drums without a big studio.

Recording drums is hard. You need thousands of dollars of microphones and recording equipment. And if you want a big sound, you need a big expensive live room. Drum replacement software can turn your drums into trigger tracks for using samples. But by capturing only timing and velocity, these solutions are like bad photo copies of a color photograph. Sensory Percussion is in full color, allowing you to capture the essense of your performance in a visually intuitive format - decades beyond MIDI.

You can record studio-quality drum tracks anywhere (bedrooms and garages work great) and have unlimited control of sounds in post production.

  Bringing the latest academic research to music creation.

Sensory Percussion's aim is to bring back the physicality of music creation from the realm of button controllers, click-tracks and procedural interfaces. We want to shift the music industry away from a mechanized and rigid recording and performance practice, back to an expressive and more human way of making music. Our first effort is with drumming, but our vision extends to other instruments and music creation software in general. We're working to reverse the trend of musicians listening to machines and create smarter systems capable of listening to and following musicians, pushing the musical instrument forward into the 21st century.
